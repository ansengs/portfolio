I'm Andrew — a self-taught developer, artist, and systems thinker from Wooster, OH. My work lives at the intersection of engineering precision and creative craft: building automation systems by day, sculpting worlds out of clay, circuitboards, and polygons in every spare moment.

I don't separate art from engineering — both demand the same methodical planning, obsessive attention to detail, and drive to make something that works and looks like it means something.

01 / Origins

My first serious coding project was a YouTube channel download manager — a month-long Python build that automated downloading copyright-free channels, then organized and filtered every video with metadata. No tutorial walked me through it. That's how I've always worked: pick a hard problem, plan it out, ship it.

In the same spirit, my first portrait (shown in the project gallery) was drawn in a single 10-hour session during my Junior year of high school — no formal training, just study and stubbornness. Graphic arts class gave me foundation in sculpture and drawing. Everything else I taught myself.
